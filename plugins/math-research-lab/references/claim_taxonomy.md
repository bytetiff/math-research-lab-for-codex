# Claim Taxonomy

- Definition: introduces an object, term, metric, protocol, or convention.
- Empirical claim: reports an observed result under a protocol.
- Metric result: reports a computed measurement or aggregation.
- Mechanistic explanation: claims a process or pathway produced an effect.
- Theoretical claim: states a theorem-like, proof-like, convergence, equivalence, or implication claim.
- Implementation claim: concerns code, configuration, checkpoint, evaluator, hardware, or artifact behavior.
- Speculation: plausible interpretation without direct operational evidence.
- Candidate concept: a provisional framing requiring disambiguation and judge review before stronger use.

A single paragraph may contain several claim types. Split them before judging evidence.
