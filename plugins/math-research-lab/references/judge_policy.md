# Judge Policy

The judge prevents two errors at once:

1. Unsupported overclaim: correlation as causation, metric decomposition as mechanism, implementation artifact as theory, scope inflation, or novelty without literature validation.
2. Unsupported downgrade: generic skepticism, vague artifact concerns, rejection from missing evidence alone, or weakening that ignores positive evidence.

Rules:

- No unsupported overclaim.
- No unsupported downgrade.
- Missing evidence lowers confidence; contradictory evidence challenges validity.
- Any alternative explanation must preserve and explain already supported observations.
- Rejection requires contradiction, failed falsification, or lack of operational definition.
