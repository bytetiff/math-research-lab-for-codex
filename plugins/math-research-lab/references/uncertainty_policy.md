# Uncertainty Policy

Use precise uncertainty wording.

- Missing evidence: required measurement is absent; confidence is capped, but the claim is not rejected.
- Contradictory evidence: a measured result conflicts with a prediction of the claim.
- Artifact-risk: a concrete alternative pathway could produce the same observation.
- Scope mismatch: the evidence supports a narrower claim than the wording states.

Do not write generic uncertainty such as "more work is needed" unless the next decisive measurement is named.
