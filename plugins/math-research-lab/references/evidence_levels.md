# Evidence Levels

Use the research-judge scorecard as the canonical scale.

- Level 0: unsupported speculation.
- Level 1: plausible interpretation.
- Level 2: correlational evidence or valid diagnostic measurement.
- Level 3: controlled ablation evidence.
- Level 4: direct mechanistic measurement.
- Level 5: replicated mechanism plus scope checks and literature validation when novelty is claimed.

Diagnostic findings can be accepted as measurements without establishing causation. Mechanism claims normally need direct measurement. Novelty claims need literature validation.
