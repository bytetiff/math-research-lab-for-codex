# Evidence Scorecard

## Evidence Levels

Level 0: unsupported speculation
No direct evidence or operational definition.

Level 1: plausible interpretation
Fits the observation but does not distinguish alternatives.

Level 2: correlational evidence
Quantitative association supports the interpretation, but causal alternatives remain open.

Level 3: controlled ablation evidence
A targeted intervention changes the result in the predicted direction.

Level 4: mechanistic measurement
The proposed mechanism is directly measured and predicts the metric change.

Level 5: replicated mechanism
Evidence holds across relevant protocols, datasets, seeds, orders, budgets, and nearby baselines.

## Confidence Labels

- high: level 4 or 5, with no major unresolved alternative.
- medium: level 3, or level 4 with scope limitations.
- low: level 1 or 2.
- unsupported: level 0.

## Evidence Direction Labels

- supporting
- neutral
- missing
- contradictory
- artifact-risk

Missing evidence is not contradictory evidence.

Diagnostic findings can be accepted without establishing causation when presented only as measurements.

A mechanism claim needs direct measurement.

A novelty claim needs literature validation.

Contradictory ablation cap:

If a controlled ablation directly contradicts a mechanism-specific claim, cap that claim at level 1 or 2 until a narrower condition explains the contradiction.

## Claim-Strength Thresholds

- A diagnostic decomposition may be accepted at level 2 or higher if presented only as a measurement.
- A mechanism claim normally requires level 4 evidence.
- A method-component causal claim requires mechanism-isolating ablations and should not exceed level 3 until direct measurements support it.
- A novelty claim requires level 5 evidence plus literature validation.
