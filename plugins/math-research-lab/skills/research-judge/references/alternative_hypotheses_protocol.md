# Alternative Hypotheses Protocol

Generate alternative hypotheses only after judging the current claim.
The goal is not brainstorming.
The goal is to find explanations that could make the preferred interpretation weaker, narrower, unnecessary, or more precise.

Every alternative hypothesis must contain:

- weakness addressed
- alternative hypothesis
- difference from current hypothesis
- supported evidence it also explains
- minimum decisive experiment
- falsifying result
- type

Allowed types:

- metric-level
- protocol-level
- optimization-level
- representation-level
- data-level
- implementation-level
- mechanism-level
- methodological
- diagnostic
- theoretical
- novelty-level

Each full judge report should consider applicable alternatives from more than one class.

No alternative may be included without a falsifying result and an explanation of which supported observations it preserves.
